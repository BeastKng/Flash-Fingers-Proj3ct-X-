The term "anime" is derived from the English word "animation" but is used to refer specifically to Japanese animated works.
Hayao Miyazaki, the co-founder of Studio Ghibli, is renowned for creating some of the most beloved and critically acclaimed anime films, such as "Spirited Away" and "My Neighbor Totoro."
"Naruto," one of the most popular anime series, was originally a manga created by Masashi Kishimoto before it was adapted into an anime.
The longest-running anime series is "Sazae-san," which has been airing since 1969 and has over 7,000 episodes.
The term "mecha" refers to anime and manga featuring giant robots, with classics like "Mobile Suit Gundam" being prominent examples.
The anime "Death Note" explores moral dilemmas through a battle of wits between a high school student and a supernatural notebook.
"Dragon Ball Z" is known for its intense battles and iconic power-up transformations, such as Super Saiyan.
The "One Piece" anime is based on a manga by Eiichiro Oda and follows the adventures of Monkey D. Luffy and his crew as they search for the ultimate treasure, One Piece.
Studio Ghibli's "Grave of the Fireflies" is a powerful and heartbreaking anti-war film that tells the story of two siblings during World War II.
"Neon Genesis Evangelion" is celebrated for its complex and psychological themes, as well as its impact on the mecha genre.
